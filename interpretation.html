Understanding Your IntelliTest Score

Your test results provide valuable insights into your cognitive abilities. Each test is scored based on specific performance criteria, allowing you to track your strengths and areas that need improvement.

Scoring Breakdown:

  • Memory Game: Your score is based on the number of moves taken to solve the grid. Fewer moves indicate better memory and pattern recognition.
  • Attention Test: Evaluates response time and accuracy in identifying patterns and sequences. A higher score reflects stronger focus and cognitive processing speed.
  • Resilience Test: Categorized into Low, Medium, and High Resilience based on responses. Higher resilience scores indicate better adaptability, problem-solving, and emotional regulation.
  • Deduction Quiz: Assesses logical reasoning and problem-solving ability. Your score reflects how efficiently you can infer, analyze, and draw conclusions from the given information.

What Do Your Scores Mean?

  • High Score: Strong cognitive ability in that area.
  • Medium Score: Decent performance, but with room for improvement.
  • Low Score: Indicates areas that need more focus and practice.

How Can You Improve?

  • Practice regularly with the practice mode before taking the final test.
  • Analyze your results to identify weaker areas and work on improving them.
  • Challenge yourself by reattempting tests to see how your cognitive skills develop over time.
